We farm along the North fork of the Skagit River outside of La Conner, Washington. Most of what we produce supports our family restaurant in Ballard, San Fermo.

Our Farm

34 acres on the Skagit River

We manage orchards, pastures, a market garden, and woodlands. We raise sheep, cattle, hogs, and free range fowl. The farm has been in the family for generations, and it's the backbone of everything we cook at San Fermo.

Most of what we grow and raise goes directly to the restaurant. What's left stocks our seasonal farm stand, where neighbors and visitors can pick up prepared foods, fresh produce, and farm products.

In summer, we open the farm for dinners, picnics, and visits. It's the best way to see where your food comes from and spend an evening in a place that feels a long way from the city.

Livestock

Sheep, cattle, Duroc hogs, and free range poultry. All raised on pasture, processed on-site or at local facilities.

Orchard & Garden

Apples, stone fruit, seasonal vegetables, herbs, flowers, and root crops. The market garden drives much of what appears on the San Fermo menu.

Preserved & Prepared

Pear and apple butter, preserves, pickles, herb salts, onion jam, and calendula healing salve. Available at the farm stand when open.

Farm Dinners

Seasonal dinners in the garden

San Fermo hosts farm dinners on select weekends from May through September. The afternoon starts with an arrival reception and hors d'oeuvres, followed by walking tours of the farm. Then we sit down for a five-course paired wine dinner in the garden. The evening finishes with dessert and coffee.

Menus are driven by what's available from the farm each season. Our chef receives a fresh sheet a few weeks before each event and builds the menu from there. All 26 guests are served the same meal, featuring our lamb, beef, pork, and produce from the garden and orchards.

Events start between 3:30 and 4:30 depending on the time of year and generally end between 7 and 8 PM.

If you have dietary restrictions, please reach out before purchasing tickets. Unlike restaurant dining, options for course substitutions are limited.

Farm Dinner FAQ

Good to know

All 26 guests are served the same meal. Options for substitutions are very limited. If you have a restricted diet, please reach out before purchasing tickets.
All bookings are final following payment confirmation. You're welcome to transfer your tickets to other guests. Please contact us so we can forward event details to the new guest. In the event of severe weather, we'll cancel and offer refunds or extend your ticket to a later date.
Events are held outside in a covered garden pergola, open to weather and elements. Dress appropriately for the season: comfortable walking shoes, warm layers in cooler months, sun protection in summer.
We seat 26 guests at three communal tables. If you're joining a larger group, let us know so we can seat you together. Groups over 8 may need to split across tables.
Due to the sensitivity of our livestock, we cannot have pets on the property.
Paired wine and bubbles are included in the ticket price. Non-alcoholic wine is available as well.
There are walking paths over rough ground and optional access to livestock areas during farm tours. Guests with significant allergies to bees, wasps, or other stinging insects are encouraged to take appropriate precautions.
